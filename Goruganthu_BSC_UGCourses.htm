2. Materials and Methodology

2.1 Data Source

The primary data source for this analysis is derived from web scraping Buffalo States official course catalog information from the University Registrars page2. The data spans the years 2015 to 2023, encompassing the fall and spring semesters for each academic year.

 

2.2 Data Collection

Web scraping was implemented using Python's requests library to retrieve the HTML content of the course catalog web pages. The BeautifulSoup library facilitated parsing and navigating the HTML structure, enabling the extraction of essential information such as the course reference number (CRN), catalog number (CatNum), department, year, and semester.

The data was systematically collected by iterating through the catalog pages for each academic year and semester3-20. Each department's course information was extracted, providing a comprehensive dataset for subsequent analysis.

 

2.3 Data Cleaning and Processing

The processing phase was implemented while ensuring the integrity and accuracy of the dataset. No data cleaning was necessary since the raw dataframe obtained had no missing values. The processed data was then structured into a Pandas DataFrame, facilitating efficient manipulation and analysis in subsequent stages.

 

2.4 Analysis Framework

The analysis involved grouping the data by relevant parameters, specifically by year, semester, and department. The objective was to quantify the number of unique courses offered in each department during the fall and spring semesters across the specified years. The Pandas library was instrumental in implementing the analysis framework, leveraging functionalities such as groupby operations and unique value counting to derive meaningful insights.

 

2.5 Scope of Analysis

The scope of analysis includes an exploration of trends and patterns in Buffalo State's undergraduate course offerings. The report focuses on presenting a comprehensive overview of the number of courses offered by each department during the specified academic years, providing users with valuable insights into the institution's academic landscape.

3.3 In-Depth Analysis: The Impact of COVID-19 in 2020

The year 2020 marked a pivotal moment globally with the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic. The effects of this unprecedented event reverberated across various sectors, including higher education.

Key Observations:

  • In Fall 2020, compared to previous year, there is a difference of only one department not offering courses.
  • This number increased by 2 for the Spring of 2021, probably as online course offerings rose in response to the pandemic.

       In terms of the number of course offerings, there were fewer courses both in Fall 2020 as well as Spring 2021 compared to previous years.

  • This downward trend continued till 2023.

 

The year 2020 witnessed unprecedented challenges globally due to the COVID-19 pandemic. Buffalo State College, like many educational institutions, experienced disruptions in its academic operations21. The decline in departmental participation and course offerings during this period can be attributed to the institution's response to the pandemic. Adaptations such as remote learning, altered academic schedules, and resource reallocation likely contributed to the observed trends22. The institution's resilience in navigating these challenges underscores its commitment to maintaining academic continuity in the face of external adversities.

 

3.4 Timeseries Analysis

Departments exhibit varying temporal engagements, with some consistently offering courses each year, while others participate in more sporadic patterns. This temporal diversity in participation underscores the fluidity and adaptability of the institution to changing educational needs.

Several departments established a continuous presence, contributing to the curriculum every academic year from 2015 to 2023. In contrast, some departments initiated their course offerings in later years, reflecting potential new academic initiatives or evolving departmental priorities. This variability in the temporal dimension of course offerings is a key aspect of our timeseries analysis.

Departments displaying a narrower timespan of course offerings, such as from 2015 to 2017 or 2015 to 2021, reveal distinct phases of engagement. These patterns might signify specific programmatic focuses or transient academic emphases during those years.

The introduction of courses by certain departments in 2022 is particularly noteworthy. This includes Data Science courses. This temporal alignment could signal a concerted effort to expand academic offerings, introducing fresh perspectives and areas of study to the Buffalo State curriculum.

3.5.2 Departments With Less Than 100 Total Course Sections

This section turns the spotlight on departments at Buffalo State College that have contributed less than 100 total course sections from 2015 to 2023. Utilizing a rigorous data-driven methodology, this analysis seeks to move beyond mere numerical representations and cultivate a nuanced understanding of the academic trajectories charted by these departments during the specified timeframe.

Of note, the Data Science and Analytics as well as Data Analytics and Mathematics departments have only offered 2 undergraduate course sections in the past two years.

Figure 4 Departments Offering Less Than A Total Of 100 Course Sections

Offering less than 100 courses over a span of nine years could indicate several noteworthy aspects about a department's academic profile:

  1. Specialization and Focus: Departments with a limited number of courses may signify a focused academic approach, emphasizing specialization in particular subject areas. This can be indicative of a deliberate strategy to excel in a niche field.
  2. Resource Allocation: A lower course count may suggest efficient resource allocation, directing faculty expertise and resources towards a select set of courses. This targeted approach can lead to high-quality instruction and student engagement in specific subject domains.
  3. Strategic Planning: Departments offering a modest number of courses may be engaging in strategic planning, aligning their academic offerings with institutional goals, student demand, and faculty expertise. This intentional approach ensures that each course serves a clear purpose within the department's overall objectives.
  4. Faculty Expertise: A focused course selection could reflect the department's faculty expertise. Departments may choose to concentrate on areas where their faculty members excel, ensuring a high standard of instruction and academic rigor.
  5. Collaborative Initiatives: Departments with fewer courses may engage in collaborative initiatives with other departments or interdisciplinary programs. This collaborative approach could lead to innovative and cross-disciplinary courses that contribute to a well-rounded educational experience.
  6. Adaptability: Offering fewer courses might indicate an ability to adapt to changing educational landscapes. Departments may streamline their offerings to meet evolving student needs, industry demands, or emerging trends in academia.

Alternative Perspective: Implications of Limited Course Offerings:

  1. Resource Allocation: Departments offering fewer courses may face resource constraints, impacting faculty availability, infrastructure, or funding. This raises questions about the equitable distribution of resources across departments.
  2. Program Viability: Limited course offerings may indicate challenges in sustaining or expanding academic programs within a department. This could prompt a closer evaluation of the program's viability, relevance, and alignment with student demand.
  3. Diversity of Offerings: A low number of course sections may limit students' choices and exposure to a variety of subjects. Departments with fewer offerings might benefit from exploring ways to diversify their course catalog to cater to diverse academic interests.
  4. Impact on Student Engagement: The availability of a broad range of courses enhances students' academic experiences. Departments offering fewer courses may need to assess how this impacts student engagement, satisfaction, and overall academic enrichment.
  5. Strategic Planning: Departments with limited course offerings may need to engage in strategic planning to address the root causes. This could involve exploring new academic collaborations, seeking external funding, or re-evaluating the department's academic focus.
  6. Impact on Departmental Reputation: A consistently low number of course sections may influence the perceived academic robustness of a department. This aspect is crucial for attracting prospective students, faculty, and fostering a positive reputation within the institution.

It might be worthwhile to assess both perspectives and all above factors in the broader context of the institution's goals, the specific academic mission of the department, and the preferences and needs of the student population. A lower course count does not inherently imply a lack of impact or significance; instead, it may underscore a department's strategic and intentional approach to academic delivery.

4. Summary

The exploration of Buffalo State College's undergraduate academic landscape over the years has unearthed intricate patterns, revealing the dynamic interplay of departments and courses. Key observations highlight the academic prowess of the Music Department, consistently leading with the highest course sectionsa testament to its enduring role in shaping the institution's educational narrative.

Fluctuations in departmental participation suggest potential shifts in academic focus, with notable drops in 2018 and 2022. The total number of courses exhibits a steady decline, emphasizing the dynamic nature of the curriculum and potential considerations like minimal enrollment requirements.

Noteworthy instances punctuate specific years, such as DAM's deliberate choice of maintaining a minimal yet impactful presence in 2020 and 2021. In contrast, the DSA department strategically expanded its course offerings in 2022 and 2023, signaling a deliberate effort to diversify its academic contributions. Examining the temporal dimension unveils consistent pioneers and diverse engagement. Some departments sustain leadership in academic contributions, displaying adaptability and robust offerings throughout the entire timeframe. Concurrently, varied engagement spans, such as from 2015 to 2017 or 2015 to 2021, denote distinct phases of departmental involvement, revealing potential programmatic focuses or transient academic emphases.

This comprehensive analysis delves into Buffalo State's academic curriculum, unraveling trends, patterns, and shifts that contribute to a nuanced understanding of the institution's educational evolution. As Buffalo State continues its journey, these insights might provide a foundation for informed decision-making, strategic planning, student academic planning and career development, and fostering a vibrant academic environment.
